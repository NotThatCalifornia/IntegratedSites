Standalone modules

If you only need to automate a single task around the brewery, all modules work on their own. They won't be connected to the cloud and there are no hidden fees.

Cloud enabled

Local server

COMING SOON! Q2 we will be launching a local monitoring station that will allow you to display your brewery state from kettle, through heat exchanger to fermentation vessels. You will also be able to compare your brews side by side to ensure you're brewing a consistent product.

Power Cost Projectings

Know how much electrity you use on each batch

We have started building our brewery during the pandemic, cost of energy hiked and then were hit by the cost of living crisis. That's why all our modules (where it makes sense), will offer an optional addon to measure exact power consumption giving you a better idea of what are the costs of each batch of beer.

Monitor and control exactly how much work has gone in the kettle and stop the transfer right on the money. Optional valve on the sparge water can even stop any new water from coming ince you done.

After a very long day brewing, chilling your wort can take forever. When is the last time you've even seen your kids? :)
With the heat exchanger controller you get three temperature sensors, flow meter and an electricly actuated water valve. All these working together will give you the readings on temperature of water coming in, wort in and wort out. All these readings will give you an idea when will you finish and een better, wen you done chlling, the electronically managed butterfly valves (* optional, connections present) can start a transfer automatically into your fermenter.
When the kettle is empty, pump turns off and valves close, all ready to be disconnected in the morning while you already in bed monitoring everything from your phone.

  • Automatic transfer when work is chilled, set and forget
  • Programable hop rests (temperature & time)
  • 1x transfer pump relay
  • 2x sanitary valve control for automatic transfer
  • 2x sanitary actuated valves *
  • Free firmware updates for life as they become available

If you are using an InkBird to manage your fermenters temperature, you might want to use one chiller (like MAXI310) to cool multiple fermenters. For this usecase we have a "not too smart" box that will automatically allow coolant to flow through your lines when the chiller mode of your InkBird is triggered.
